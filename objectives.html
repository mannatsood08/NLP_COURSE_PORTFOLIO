Course Learning Objectives

The curriculum is organized into five distinct units, each addressing specific aspects of Natural Language Processing. Each unit contains defined learning outcomes and comprehensive topic coverage.

Unit 1: Overview and Word Level Analysis

9 Hours

Foundation of NLP concepts and word-level processing techniques

  • Understand the fundamental concepts and applications of NLP
  • Master text preprocessing techniques including tokenization, stemming, and lemmatization
  • Learn feature extraction methods such as TF-IDF and implement POS tagging
View Details

Unit 2: Syntax Analysis

9 Hours

Understanding the structure and grammar of language

  • Apply Context Free Grammars and parsing techniques to analyze sentence structure
  • Identify and resolve syntactic ambiguity in natural language
  • Implement dependency parsing for relationship extraction
View Details

Unit 3: Semantic and Discourse Analysis

9 Hours

Extracting meaning from text and understanding context

  • Explore word sense disambiguation and lexical semantics
  • Implement word embedding techniques like Word2Vec and GloVe
  • Apply discourse segmentation and coreference resolution
View Details

Unit 4: Language Models

9 Hours

Advanced neural architectures for language understanding

  • Understand and implement RNN and LSTM architectures
  • Master transformer models including self-attention mechanisms
  • Fine-tune pre-trained models like BERT for specific NLP tasks
View Details

Unit 5: NLP Applications

9 Hours

Practical implementation of NLP in real-world scenarios

  • Build chatbots and information extraction systems
  • Implement text summarization techniques (extractive and abstractive)
  • Develop question answering systems and machine translation models
