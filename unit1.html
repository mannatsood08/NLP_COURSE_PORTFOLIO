Unit 1 — Overview and Word Level Analysis

Duration: 9 Hours

This unit provides a foundational understanding of Natural Language Processing, introducing key concepts and techniques for analyzing text at the word level. These fundamental skills form the basis for more advanced NLP applications.

Topics Covered

  • Introduction to NLP: History, challenges, and current state of the field
  • Applications of NLP: Text classification, sentiment analysis, machine translation, and more
  • Levels of NLP: Phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, pragmatics, and discourse
  • Regular Expressions: Pattern matching for text processing
  • Morphological Analysis: Understanding word structure and formation
  • Tokenization: Breaking text into meaningful units
  • Stemming: Reducing words to their root form
  • Lemmatization: Converting words to their dictionary form
  • Feature extraction: TF (Term Frequency), IDF (Inverse Document Frequency), TF-IDF
  • POS Tagging: Identifying parts of speech in text
  • Named Entity Recognition: Identifying entities like people, organizations, locations
  • N-grams: Analyzing sequences of n words
  • Smoothing: Handling unseen data in language models

Key Takeaways

In this unit, I learned how to preprocess text data for NLP tasks, extract meaningful features from text, and apply basic linguistic analysis techniques. These skills are essential for any NLP practitioner and form the foundation for more advanced techniques covered in later units.
